# About

The **Eurasian wryneck** or **northern wryneck** (***Jynx torquilla***) is a species of [wryneck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wryneck) in the [woodpecker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woodpecker) [family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Family_(taxonomy)).

## Habitat:

* *Jynx torquilla torquilla*

   Linnaeus, 1758 — most of Europe (except for areas occupied by 

  *J. t. tschusii*

  ), south to Turkey and the Caucasus, wintering in tropical Africa and India
* *Jynx torquilla sarudnyi*

   Loudon, 1912 — central & western Siberia, from the Urals east to the Yenisei, wintering in southern Asia

## **Behaviour**:

1. The Eurasian wryneck sometimes forms small groups during migration and in its winter quarters but in the summer is usually found in pairs.
2. It characteristically holds its head high with its beak pointing slightly upwards. 

   Sometimes the head is allowed to slump sideways and hang limply.
